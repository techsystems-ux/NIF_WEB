Diploma in Fashion Designing after 10th: Duration, Fees, Eligibility and Career Scope in India

June 17, 2026 8 min read

A diploma in fashion designing is one of the most popular early entry points into the fashion industry for students who want to start building design skills right after completing their 10th standard. It offers a shorter, more focused alternative to longer degree programs while still covering the core foundations of fashion design. This guide breaks down what the course involves, who can apply, what it costs, and where it can lead.

What Is a Diploma in Fashion Designing?

A diploma in fashion designing is a foundational course that introduces students to the basics of fashion design, including sketching, fabric selection, color theory, pattern making, and garment construction. It focuses on the technical aspects of apparel design and fabrication, covering topics such as design concepts, garment construction techniques, and fashion illustration. Compared to a full degree program, a diploma is shorter in duration and more focused on practical, entry-level skills, making it a popular choice for students who want to start their fashion journey early.

Eligibility for Diploma in Fashion Designing after 10th

To apply for a diploma in fashion designing after 10th, applicants generally need to have completed at least class 10th from a recognized board. Some institutes also specify a minimum percentage requirement, typically around 45 to 50% in Class 10, though this varies by college. Most diploma courses do not require an entrance exam and instead focus on basic eligibility along with the candidate's interest in design, making the course accessible to students from a wide range of academic backgrounds.

Diploma in Fashion Designing Duration

A diploma in fashion designing after 10th is usually a one-year course, though some colleges also offer two-year diploma programs. Depending on the institute and specialization, diploma programs in this field can range from one to three years. The shorter duration compared to degree courses makes it a practical option for students looking to enter the workforce or pursue further studies more quickly.

Diploma in Fashion Designing Fees

Fees for a diploma in fashion designing vary widely depending on the institute, location, and course structure. Government colleges typically charge lower fees, ranging from around ₹5,000 to ₹20,000, while private colleges can charge anywhere from ₹10,000 to ₹2,00,000 for the full program. Some sources place the average total fees for a diploma program between ₹1,00,000 and ₹4,00,000, reflecting the difference between basic and more comprehensive, industry-exposure-heavy programs.

What You Learn in a Diploma in Fashion Designing

  • General fashion theory and history of costumes
  • Fashion tools, techniques, and accessories
  • Fashion forecasting and core design principles
  • Stitching methods and practical garment construction
  • Hands-on projects in sketching, draping, and basic construction to build a foundational portfolio

Career Scope after Diploma in Fashion Designing

After completing a diploma in fashion designing, candidates can explore roles such as Fashion Buyer, Visual Merchandiser, Brand Manager, Quality Controller, Retail Manager, and Pattern Maker, among others. Some graduates choose to gain work experience directly in these roles, while others use the diploma as a stepping stone toward further studies, such as a bachelor's degree in fashion design, to access more advanced design and creative roles. With the fashion and lifestyle sector in India growing steadily, there is an increasing need for professionals with foundational design skills across retail, styling, and production.

Many of these roles serve as practical starting points for students entering the workforce directly after their diploma, offering exposure to retail operations, production processes, or merchandising workflows. This hands-on experience often helps graduates decide which direction within fashion design suits them best, whether that's staying in a specialized operational role or eventually pursuing further education to move into design-focused positions.

Diploma in Fashion Designing Salary

The average salary after completing a one-year diploma in fashion designing is typically around ₹2 to 3 lakhs per annum, while those completing a shorter certificate course may start around ₹1.5 to 2 lakhs per annum. A career after this diploma generally offers an average annual income in the range of ₹2 to 3 lakhs, with salaries increasing as candidates gain experience or pursue additional qualifications. Entry-level roles in retail, merchandising, or production support are common starting points, with growth potential as skills and experience develop.

Is a Diploma Enough, or Should You Pursue a Degree?

A diploma in fashion designing is a useful starting point, especially for students who want early exposure to the field or are unsure about committing to a longer program right away. However, for students aiming for more advanced design roles, creative leadership positions, or a stronger foundation in design thinking, a structured fashion design course such as a BDes or BVoc program offers more in-depth learning and a broader skill set. Many students use a diploma as an initial step before progressing to a full degree program for long-term career growth.
