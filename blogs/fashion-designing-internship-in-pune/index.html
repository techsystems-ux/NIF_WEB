Fashion Designing Internship in Pune: How to Get One and What to Expect

June 17, 2026 8 min read

A fashion designing internship is a structured, short-term work opportunity where students or recent graduates gain hands-on experience in a real design environment. It allows learners to apply classroom concepts to actual projects, understand industry workflows, and build professional connections before starting a full-time career. For students based in or around Pune, internships offer a practical way to bridge the gap between academic learning and the demands of the fashion industry.

Eligibility for a Fashion Design Internship in Pune

Most fashion design internships in Pune require candidates to be enrolled in or have completed a diploma, BVoc, or BDes program in fashion design. Some companies also accept students from textile design, fashion merchandising, or related backgrounds. Basic knowledge of garment construction, sketching, and design software such as Adobe Illustrator or CorelDRAW is often preferred. Internships may be open to current students fulfilling academic requirements as well as freshers looking to build experience.

How to Get a Fashion Designing Internship in Pune

  • Apply through college placement cells, online job portals, and direct outreach to design studios, boutiques, and garment export houses.
  • Prepare a strong application: updated resume, portfolio showcasing design work, and a short cover note on interest and availability.
  • Network through industry events, workshops, and fashion shows — many positions are filled through referrals.
  • Follow design studios and brands on social media to catch openings not listed on major job portals.

Portfolio Ideas for Fashion Design Students

A portfolio is one of the most important tools when applying for a fashion design internship. It should include a mix of sketches, mood boards, fabric swatches, and finished garment photographs. Students can also include digital design work, technical drawings (flats), and project documentation showing the design process from concept to final product. A well-organized portfolio, whether physical or digital, demonstrates creativity, technical skill, and attention to detail. Including a short personal statement about design philosophy or inspiration can also help a portfolio stand out.

Resume Format for Fashion Designers

A fashion designer's resume should be concise and visually clean, typically one to two pages. Key sections include contact information, a brief summary or objective, educational qualifications, design software proficiency, key projects or coursework, and any prior internship or work experience. For freshers, highlighting academic projects, participation in fashion shows, and relevant skills such as pattern making, draping, or illustration can strengthen the resume even without formal work experience. A clean layout with clear section headings makes it easier for recruiters to quickly identify relevant information.

The table below summarizes the typical sections to include in a fashion design resume and what each should cover.

Key Sections of a Fashion Designer's Resume
Resume Section What to Include
Contact Information Full name, phone number, a professional email address, and a link to your portfolio or LinkedIn.
Summary / Objective A brief two to three line statement on your design focus, strengths, and career goals.
Education Degree or diploma, institute name, and year of completion or expected graduation.

What to Expect During the Internship

During a fashion design internship, interns typically assist with tasks such as sketching designs, sourcing fabrics and trims, preparing mood boards, supporting sample development, and assisting with production coordination. Depending on the organization, interns may also be involved in client interactions, trend research, or visual merchandising. The work environment is usually fast-paced, and interns are expected to learn quickly while supporting the design team's ongoing projects. Interns may work closely with senior designers, which offers valuable mentorship opportunities alongside day-to-day tasks.

Internship Duration and Stipend

Fashion design internships generally last between three to six months, often aligned with college internship requirements. Stipends vary based on the organization, role, and city, with entry-level internships in Pune commonly ranging from a few thousand rupees per month. Some internships are unpaid, particularly those offering significant learning exposure or mentorship from established designers and studios.

Fashion Design Internship in India

Fashion design internships are available across major cities in India, including Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Pune. Each city offers different types of opportunities depending on its design ecosystem, ranging from large garment manufacturing units to boutique design studios and emerging designer labels. Pune, with its growing design institutes and proximity to Mumbai's fashion industry, offers a mix of local and pan-India internship opportunities for students.

How Much Does a Fashion Designer Earn?

Entry-level fashion designers in India typically earn between ₹2,00,000 to ₹4,00,000 per annum, depending on the role, location, and type of organization. Salaries can increase significantly with experience, specialization, and a strong portfolio. Designers working with established brands, export houses, or starting their own labels may see higher earning potential as their career progresses.

The internship experience gained early on often plays a direct role in shaping these early career outcomes. Interns who take on varied tasks, build a strong portfolio, and develop industry connections during their internship tend to have an easier transition into entry-level roles, sometimes even receiving job offers from the same organization. For students in Pune, a well-utilized internship can serve as both a learning experience and a stepping stone toward better-paying opportunities as they begin their careers in fashion design.

Frequently Asked Questions

What skills are needed for a fashion design internship?

Most fashion design internships require basic sketching ability, an understanding of fabrics and garment construction, and familiarity with design software such as Adobe Illustrator or CorelDRAW. Communication skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work well within a team are also valued. Time management is important too, since interns often handle multiple tasks alongside design teams during fast-paced project cycles in studios or production units.

Can freshers apply for fashion design internships?

Yes, freshers can apply for fashion design internships, as many positions are designed specifically for students or recent graduates with limited work experience. The focus is typically on learning, skill development, and gaining exposure to real industry workflows. A strong portfolio and resume highlighting academic projects, fashion show participation, and relevant coursework can help freshers stand out during the application process.

Are fashion design internships paid?

Fashion design internships can be either paid or unpaid, depending on the organization, role, and duration. Paid internships usually offer a monthly stipend, while unpaid internships often focus on mentorship, learning, and industry exposure. Students should clarify stipend details, working hours, and expectations with the employer before accepting an internship offer to avoid any confusion later on.

How long does a fashion design internship usually last?

Fashion design internships typically last between three to six months, often aligned with college academic requirements or semester schedules. Some organizations may offer shorter or longer durations depending on project needs and intern performance. Students should confirm the exact duration, working days, and any extension possibilities with the employer beforehand to plan their academic and professional commitments accordingly.

What should be included in a fashion design portfolio for internships?

A strong fashion design portfolio should include sketches, mood boards, fabric swatches, and photographs of finished garments. Technical drawings, digital design work, and documentation showing the design process from concept to completion are also valuable additions. A short personal statement about design philosophy or inspiration can further help a portfolio stand out to potential employers or internship providers.
